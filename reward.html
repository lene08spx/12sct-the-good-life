What is the Catholic understanding of salvation?

Enter to heaven for the afterlife. Heaven is a gift from God granted through our salvation by Jesus' death and resurection. Jesus provides a view of hope: that it is a surrender of control but an exercise of perfect trust bringing a welcoming and gracious divine providence (Righteousness of God).

What is meant by judgement?

The judgement of the human soul of someone who has died, it realises either eternal salvation or damnation. Judgement is when a person is judged for the goodness of their life, and comprises of two main types of judgement: one at the end of life, and one at the end of time. To be in heaven is eternity in Christ's presence. To be in hell is an eternity without.

How are the teachings of Jesus linked to salvation?

Jesus teaches the ideas and values that a person should live out in order to have a good and fulfilling life. Jesus tried explaining to the Jews the real understanding of a good life, showing that a good life is about sacrifice, love, kindness, and service to others, and not about strict adherence to rituals.

Linking to the...

Good Life

A secular view of the reward of a good life includes recognition by others and feeling good about yourself. The Catholic view is similar, but instead God will recognise one’s works and reward that person in heaven. The secular view ends when a person dies. The Catholic view continues into the afterlife. The afterlife is preceded by judgement. This appears in Revelations. Jesus teaches by demonstrating kindness, love, compassion and service. We see Jesus ascend into heaven, as a reward for his suffering on for humanity on Earth.

Fall

Despite Adam and Eve sinning, we see they recognise their mistakes and are rewarded with forgiveness. The fall relates to the reward in that through learning from our selfishness, we are rewarded by God. A secular view takes the view of learning lessons from your mistakes, but the only reward is that you don’t repeat it again.

Faith

Faith in God to reward one. Revelations. God is the most kind and forgiving force in the universe. If we can live a good life in God’s eyes, then we have truly lived a good life on Earth. It is about service to God and others, that allows one to not become overpowered by selfish desire. Overcoming this desire is what enable one to escape evil, the void of all good. This selfish desire is an animalistic tendency, and for us to truly live a good human life, one must overcome temptation, as in the story of the serpent.
