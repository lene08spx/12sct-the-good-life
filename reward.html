What is the Catholic understanding of salvation?

Enter to heaven for the afterlife. Heaven is a gift from God granted through our salvation by Jesus' death and resurection. Jesus provides a view of hope: that it is a surrender of control but an exercise of perfect trust bringing a welcoming and gracious divine providence (Righteousness of God).

What is meant by judgement?

The judgement of the human soul of someone who has died, it realises either eternal salvation or damnation. Judgement is when a person is judged for the goodness of their life, and comprises of two main types of judgement: one at the end of life, and one at the end of time. To be in heaven is eternity in Christ's presence. To be in hell is an eternity without.

How are the teachings of Jesus linked to salvation?

Jesus teaches the ideas and values that a person should live out in order to have a good and fulfilling life. Jesus tried explaining to the Jews the real understanding of a good life, showing that a good life is about sacrifice, love, kindness, and service to others, and not about strict adherence to rituals.

Linking to the...

Good Life

A secular view of the reward of a good life includes recognition by others and feeling good about yourself. The Catholic view is similar, but instead God will recognise one’s works and reward that person in heaven. The secular view ends when a person dies. The Catholic view continues into the afterlife. The afterlife is preceded by judgement, which is described in Corinthians 5:10 and Revelations 20:11-15. Jesus teaches that entering into heaven is the strongest test of our relationship with God, being an event of perfect trust but it is welcoming, and opens up a divine understanding of the world to us. The reward for demonstrating a life of kindness, love, compassion and service is shown in Jesus ascension in Acts 1:6-11. Jesus ascension into heaven is a reward for and relief from his suffering for humanity on Earth so that we may be saved at the end of time.

Fall

The story of St Paul descibes the reward for a Good Life. Despite his original life of sin persecuting Christians, we see him transform (Acts 9: 1-19) into a man for the service of God. For his recognition of what he has done wrong, God rewarded him with forgiveness and a second chance. The reward is for the Good Life of extraordinary service of Paul, in his travel to spread the Good News, which is evidence by the many letters from Paul to groups around the Mediterranean. This story demonstrates the Catholic view that in learning from our selfishness, we are rewarded by God. A secular view takes the view of learning lessons from your mistakes, but the only reward is that you don’t repeat it again.

Faith

A Good Life of Faith, is one trusting in God. God is the most kind and forgiving force in the universe. If we can live a good life in God’s eyes, then we have truly lived a good life on Earth. It is about service to God and others, that allows one to not become overpowered by selfish desire. Overcoming this desire is what enables one to escape evil, the void of all good. This selfish desire is an animalistic tendency, and for us to truly live a good human life, one must overcome temptation, as in the story of the serpent (Genesis 3) and The Golden Calf (Exodus 32).
